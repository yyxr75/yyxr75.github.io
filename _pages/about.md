I am a PhD researcher at [James Cook University](https://www.jcu.edu.au/), advised by [Dr. Tao Huang](https://research.jcu.edu.au/portfolio/tao.huang1/) in the [Intelligent Computing & Communications (ICC) Lab](https://www.taoicclab.com/). My research centers on **diffusion-based generative priors for inverse problems and sensing** — recovering clean structure from noisy, incomplete measurements — and extends these ideas toward **world models** for perception.

Before my PhD I spent nearly four years as an algorithm engineer at Vanjee Technology in Beijing, building **radar and LiDAR perception** for autonomous driving, and I began in optical engineering with a master's on 3D thermal-surface reconstruction.

If your group works on generative modeling, computational imaging, or sensor fusion, I would be glad to connect — feel free to [email me](mailto:yanlong.yang@my.jcu.edu.au).

Research interests
======
- Diffusion / generative models for inverse problems and computational imaging
- Multi-sensor fusion (radar, LiDAR, camera) for autonomous-driving perception
- 3D object detection, tracking, and point-cloud enhancement
- World models for perception

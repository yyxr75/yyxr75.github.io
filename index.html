I’m a PhD researcher at James Cook University, advised by Dr. Tao Huang. My work centers on diffusion-based generative priors for inverse problems and sensing — recovering clean structure from noisy, incomplete measurements. My recent first-author work at CVPR 2026 (DeblurSDI) tackles blind imaging with a self-diffusion framework that needs no pre-training.

Before my PhD I spent nearly four years as an algorithm engineer at Vanjee Technology in Beijing, building radar and LiDAR perception for autonomous driving, and I began in optical engineering with a master’s on 3D thermal-surface reconstruction. I’m now moving toward world models for perception.

If your group works on generative modeling, computational imaging, or sensor fusion, I’d be glad to connect — feel free to email me.

selected publications

  1. CVPR
    Self-Diffusion Driven Blind Imaging
    Yanlong Yang, and Guanxiong Luo
    In IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) , 2026
    Abs arXiv Bib HTML

    A zero-shot, self-supervised blind imaging framework (DeblurSDI) that requires no pre-training. It formulates blind image recovery as an iterative reverse self-diffusion process, starting from pure noise and jointly refining both the sharp image and the unknown degradation kernel, for combined optical aberrations and motion blur.
